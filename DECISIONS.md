## 2026-06-15 ‚Äî Deployment: two Spaces + read audio via soundfile, not torchcodec

- **Choice:** Ship on **ZeroGPU** for the competition-compliant Space `build-small-hackathon/glossolalia` (free GPU, what the hackathon expects). Keep a second working copy at `akshan-main/glossolalia` on a paid T4 as a backup. One shared `app.py` runs on both via a guarded `@spaces.GPU` decorator (real on ZeroGPU where `spaces` is installed, a no-op pass-through on the T4 / locally). **Read reference audio with `soundfile` (libsndfile) instead of `torchaudio.load`'s torchcodec path.**
- **Rejected:** (a) torchcodec on ZeroGPU ‚Äî five dependency permutations all failed at the native-lib `dlopen`: default PyPI torchcodec wants CUDA 13 (`libnvrtc.so.13`); the cu128 build (torchcodec 0.9.1, ABI-correct for torch 2.9) needs CUDA runtime libs that don't resolve inside ZeroGPU's transient-GPU sandbox even with the FFmpeg dev libs present; the CPU torchcodec build (0.11+) isn't ABI-compatible with the torch 2.9 ZeroGPU forces. (b) Moving the Space into the org via transfer ‚Äî HF returns 402 (org has no paid-hardware billing). (c) A `try torchcodec / except soundfile` fallback wrapper ‚Äî rejected earlier as papering over the problem.
- **Why this one:** ZeroGPU is correct (the competition's free GPU, used by ~everyone); torchcodec is the part that isn't viable here. torchaudio **had** soundfile/sox/ffmpeg load backends and **removed them in 2.8** in favor of torchcodec ([pytorch/audio#3902](https://github.com/pytorch/audio/issues/3902)). Our reference voices are all WAV, so reading them with `soundfile` is the *exact* operation torchaudio's deleted soundfile backend performed (libsndfile ‚Üí tensor) ‚Äî a direct replacement, not a fallback. Installed in `patches/torchaudio_soundfile.py` before `f5_tts` imports, so torchcodec is never touched (it stays a dormant transitive dep). Verified end-to-end by live `gradio_client` `/speak` calls against both Spaces returning real audio (~1.9 s, rms ~0.16‚Äì0.19).
- **Sources:** torchcodec‚Üîtorch compatibility table ([github.com/pytorch/torchcodec](https://github.com/pytorch/torchcodec?tab=readme-ov-file#installing-torchcodec)) ‚Äî torchcodec 0.9 ‚Üî torch 2.9, py3.10-3.14; confirms ABI was fine and the blocker was the CUDA-lib load, not version mismatch. torchaudio backend removal ([pytorch/audio#3902](https://github.com/pytorch/audio/issues/3902)). ZeroGPU supported torch versions (2.8.0 / 2.9.1 / 2.10.0 / 2.11.0) ‚Äî from the Space's own CONFIG_ERROR message. HF Spaces hardware 402 on org transfer ‚Äî HF billing API.
- **Pins that matter:** ZeroGPU Space ‚Üí `torch==2.9.1`, `torchaudio==2.9.1`, `spaces`, `f5-tts==1.1.20`, `peft==0.18.1`, `gradio==6.10.0` (f5-tts requires `gradio<6.11`), python 3.10 (ZeroGPU). T4 Space ‚Üí `torch==2.7.1` + `torchaudio==2.7.1` (still has the ffmpeg load backend; soundfile patch is a harmless override there), python 3.11.
- **Workflow:** `gradio-fixed-bg-research` (Gradio DOM / fixed-background pattern, 16 agents) drove the separate background-layer fix. The torchcodec resolution was iterative live-Space debugging (5 build cycles), each diagnosed from the Space's runtime logs.

## 2026-06-15 ‚Äî Ghost mode dissolution = count-based + monotonic, real-common-word filtered

- **Choice:** In Ghost mode (mondegreen), the dial level controls **how many** content words are substituted (a deterministic count `ceil(p¬∑N)`, nested so each level adds to the previous), not a per-word random draw. Each substituted word takes its nearest real-word mishearing within a level-scaled PanPhon distance cap `[0, 3, 5, 7.5, 10]`. Candidates are filtered to **common English** words via `wordfreq` (zipf ‚â• 2.5) and de-duplicated within a sentence.
- **Rejected:** (a) The original per-word Bernoulli firing ‚Äî on short sentences it changed zero words at low dials, so levels 0/1/2 looked identical (the dial felt dead). (b) The tight distance cap `[0,1.5,3,4.5,6]` ‚Äî multi-syllable words ("seashells", "seashore") have their nearest real-word mishearing at distance ~7-8, so they passed through unchanged even at dial 4 ("she **sills** seashells" instead of full dissolution). (c) No frequency filter ‚Äî surfaced rare CMUdict junk ("selz", zipf 1.4) that isn't a real word; no dedup ‚Äî produced "cecil ‚Ä¶ cecil" repeats.
- **Why this one:** The dial must visibly + audibly progress at every step. Count-based monotonic selection guarantees lv1 ‚äÇ lv2 ‚äÇ ‚Ä¶ ‚äÇ lv4 with a strictly growing change count; the generous per-word distance cap lets long words find their nearest mishearing once selected; the zipf filter keeps outputs to ordinary English (the mondegreen/pareidolia effect needs real words); dedup stops collapse onto one frequent word. Verified: `she sells seashells by the seashore` ‚Üí lv1 `she sills seashells‚Ä¶` ‚Üí lv2 `‚Ä¶seagulls‚Ä¶` ‚Üí lv4 `she sills seagulls by the thistle`. This is inference-time only (Ghost uses no LoRA), so no retrain.
- **Sources:** `wordfreq` (Speer et al., zipf scale = log10 per-billion). PanPhon feature-edit distance ([aclanthology.org/C16-1328](https://aclanthology.org/C16-1328/)). DistilGPT-2 reranker (MIT) scores candidate sequences for coherence.
- **Note:** Ghost is **mondegreen, not glossolalia** ‚Äî real-word mishearing (pareidolia), a different aesthetic from the Tongues path. Only Tongues is "speaking in tongues." Labeled honestly in UI + BLOG.

## 2026-05-31 ‚Äî TTS base = F5-TTS (not Spark / Chatterbox / Orpheus / Higgs / ElevenLabs)

- **Choice:** F5-TTS v1 Base (~336M, flow-matching, character-level input, zero-shot voice cloning).
- **Rejected:** Spark-TTS, Chatterbox, Orpheus, Higgs Audio v2, ElevenLabs, XTTS-v2.
- **Why this one:** F5-TTS has **no LLM front-end**. LLM-backed TTS auto-corrects out-of-distribution input ‚Äî corrupted phoneme strings, the load-bearing input for the dial ‚Äî back toward real English, collapsing the trajectory to two presets with a crossfade in the middle. F5-TTS pronounces corrupted spellings as written, which is what makes the *middle* of the dial possible. Smaller-model penalty is worth paying to keep the dial coherent.
- **Sources:** F5-TTS paper (Chen et al. 2024, arXiv:2410.06885); HuggingFace model card (`SWivid/F5-TTS`).
- **Workflow:** *TTS-choice research workflow*, 2026-05-31. Adversarially verified across 11 candidate models including F5R-TTS and 2026 newcomers.

## 2026-05-31 ‚Äî Adapter = LoRA, not LoKr

- **Choice:** Plain LoRA, rank 16, alpha 16, target F5-TTS DiT attention modules (`to_q`, `to_k`, `to_v`, `to_out.0`).
- **Rejected:** LoKr, full fine-tune, prefix tuning, IA¬≥.
- **Why this one:** Concept Sliders (Gandikota et al., ECCV 2024) demonstrated that LoRA's low-rank constraint *is* the disentanglement mechanism for graded continuous control ‚Äî that's our exact problem. LoKr's "10√ó faster" claim is confounded with caching defaults and irrelevant on our cached-latent pipeline; LoKr also doesn't weight-merge, breaking the clean composition path. Prior internal proof: density-knob experiment shipped Spearman 0.90 monotonicity + cross-drift 0.10.
- **Sources:** Concept Sliders, Gandikota et al., ECCV 2024 (arXiv:2311.12092).
- **Workflow:** Internal density-knob pilot (2026-05); LoKr literature scan (2026-05).

## 2026-06-02 ‚Äî Per-level learnable conditioning tokens (textual-inversion-style), not text-control

- **Choice:** A learnable `nn.Embedding(5, 512)` (one vector per level) is attached to F5-TTS's DiT and added to the post-conv text embedding inside `DiT.get_input_embed` (verified site: `src/f5_tts/model/backbones/dit.py` L284-312). The input text is just the sentence with no control marker. The LoRA adapter and the level embeddings co-train. At inference, `F5TTS.set_dial(level)` writes the per-batch level slot and busts the CFG text cache so the next infer picks up the new bias.
- **Rejected:** (a) Text-control token (`"sentence | tongues N"`) ‚Äî the v1 attempt. Cell 7 verdict was PARTIAL, Spearman -0.70, voice cosine 0.97. Listened to Cell 8 wavs: F5-TTS read the entire input as text, so the model spoke the level word verbatim and the LoRA could not in 50 steps simultaneously silence the marker and corrupt the audio. (b) Separator-as-signal trick (special unicode that F5-TTS might ignore) ‚Äî still leaves the LoRA with the two-jobs-in-one task. (c) Scaling text-control to 7,500 clips √ó 5 epochs ‚Äî architectural problem, not under-training.
- **Why this one:** The conditioning signal is not text, so F5-TTS cannot read it aloud. The LoRA only has to learn one thing (the audio transformation per bias direction). The level embedding is six orders of magnitude smaller than the base model (5 √ó 512 = 2,560 params vs 336M); together with rank-16 LoRA on the attention projections (~2.9M params), the trainable surface stays at ~0.85% of the model.
- **Sources:** Textual Inversion, Gal et al. 2022, [arXiv:2208.01618](https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.01618) ‚Äî learnable embedding vectors in the text-encoder space, trained while the base model is frozen. The pattern transfers cleanly from Stable Diffusion's text encoder to F5-TTS's text-embedding pipeline. F5-TTS architecture: source-verified at [`src/f5_tts/model/backbones/dit.py`](https://github.com/SWivid/F5-TTS/blob/main/src/f5_tts/model/backbones/dit.py) L284-312 and `cfm.py` L294.
- **NOT cited:** Concept Sliders (Gandikota et al., ECCV 2024) ‚Äî verified at [`sliders/trainscripts/textsliders/lora.py`](https://github.com/rohitgandikota/sliders) L249, it is a SCALAR-MODULATED LORA (`org_forward(x) + lora_up(lora_down(x)) * multiplier * scale`), not a per-level embedding table. The signed-direction LECO training assumes opposing prompt pairs we do not have. Using it as precedent for our setup would be inaccurate.
- **Workflow:** *F5-TTS per-level embedding architecture*, 2026-06-02. 14 of 18 claims survived adversarial verification.

## 2026-06-12 ‚Äî Dual-mode dial: Ghost (mondegreen) + Tongues (glossolalia) under "Dreamy" umbrella

- **Choice:** Ship two corruption modes under one product:
  - **Ghost mode (`scripts/mondegreen.py`)** ‚Äî deterministic word-level substitution. For each non-function word in the input lyric, search CMUdict for real English words within PanPhon feature-edit-distance D(level), restricted to the same syllable count AND same primary-stress position as the source. Sample from candidates weighted by 1/(1+distance). Output is always a sequence of real English words; F5-TTS base reads the substituted lyric directly ‚Äî no LoRA needed for Ghost mode.
  - **Tongues mode (`scripts/corrupt_phonemes.py`)** ‚Äî the existing v8 PanPhon Boltzmann √ó dreamy_weight per-phoneme kernel. Output is invented pseudowords; the v8 LoRA + LevelEmbed adapt F5-TTS to read clean text as the graded glossolalic version of itself.
- **Schedules (Ghost mode):** D(level) ‚àà {0, 2, 4, 6, 8} raw PanPhon feature-edit-distance units; p(level) ‚àà {0.0, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 1.0} per-word substitution probability. Function words held constant (small closed-class list). Source word passes through unchanged when no candidate exists within the distance cap (graceful degradation for OOV / rare phonotactic combos like "courtyard").
- **Rejected:** (a) Cross-word boundary substitution (true soramimi-style "ella megalast ‚Üí men don't talk much" 3-words ‚Üí 4-words). Adds O(n¬≤) candidate complexity with phrase-level beam search; deferred, not ruled out. (b) Skipping stress preservation ‚Äî substituting a trochaic word with an iambic candidate breaks rhythmic alignment under the original melody. (c) Using Whisper ASR empirical confusion matrix as distance metric instead of PanPhon ‚Äî would require a one-time corpus extraction we don't have; PanPhon is the validated off-the-shelf substitute (PWESuite 2024 reports a strong correlation of PanPhon distance with human similarity judgments). (d) An LLM-driven substitution step ‚Äî covered in earlier "no-cloud / determinism" discussion; LLMs are unreliable at syllable counting and stress preservation, breaking the core constraint.
- **What is VERIFIED:**
  - PanPhon library distances measured directly: P/B=1, S/SH=2, P/M=3, P/ZH=7, K/N=8, AA/P=11. Matrix range [0, 48].
  - PWESuite ([arXiv:2304.02541](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.02541)) is a real paper titled "PWESuite: Phonetic Word Embeddings and Tasks They Facilitate" ‚Äî supports the general claim that PanPhon-style features are validated against human similarity judgments. The specific Pearson r=0.82 figure is in the synthesis but I did not personally pull the exact value from the paper, so it appears here as "strong correlation" rather than a specific number.
  - CMUdict (~135K English entries, public domain) is the canonical pronunciation dictionary used.
  - Diverse-input local test (8 sentences, 5 levels each): syllable count preserved, deterministic, function words held, OOV words pass through unchanged. Pulled examples: river‚Üíreaser, morning‚Üíboarding, light‚Üílout, imagine‚Üíemulsion, people‚Üíbiehle, began‚Üíbegot.
- **What is HEURISTIC (no false provenance):**
  - The specific distance schedule D(level) = {0, 2, 4, 6, 8} is chosen for a smooth dial. No published calibration of "ghost perceptibility threshold" against this CMUdict/PanPhon combination exists.
  - The probability schedule p(level) = {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1} matches the existing Tongues-mode schedule so the two dials feel symmetric. Hand-picked.
  - The function-word hold list is hand-curated from the standard English closed-class items (articles, prepositions, conjunctions, common pronouns and auxiliaries). It is INFORMED by ‚Äî but not derived from ‚Äî the music-cognition tradition that function words carry less perceptual weight under song-rate listening (Cutler & Norris 1988 MSS is the standard secondary reference, but I did not personally verify the citation page for this entry; the workflow synthesis cited it).
  - The stress-preservation constraint (substituted word must match source word's primary stress position) is INFORMED by published music-cognition work (the workflow synthesis cited Kolinsky et al. + EEG study PMC3225926 as evidence that stress mismatch degrades song-word recognition). I did not personally fetch those papers for this entry; the citations are surfaced honestly as "cited in workflow synthesis, not personally re-verified."
- **Sources I personally verified:** PanPhon (Mortensen et al. COLING 2016, [aclanthology.org/C16-1328](https://aclanthology.org/C16-1328/)) ‚Äî used as software; PWESuite (Sabbaqi & Cotterell, LREC-COLING 2024, [arXiv:2304.02541](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.02541)) ‚Äî title and existence verified; CMUdict ([github.com/cmusphinx/cmudict](https://github.com/cmusphinx/cmudict)) ‚Äî used as data.
- **Sources surfaced by workflow synthesis, not personally re-verified for this entry:** Cutler & Norris 1988 MSS (function-word salience); Baese-Berk et al. 2016 (PMC5014323); Kolinsky et al. *Empirical Musicology Review* (stress-mismatch song recognition); EEG study PMC3225926; SimPhon 2025 ([arXiv:2506.11620](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11620)). If we cite any of these in BLOG.md or demo voiceover, I or the author must personally read them first.
- **AI load-bearing under dual-mode:** Tongues mode preserves the strong AI claim ‚Äî the v8 LoRA + LevelEmbed condition F5-TTS to produce graded glossolalia from clean text + a scalar; Concept-Sliders-style scalar control over a flow-matching TTS is novel. Ghost mode is more deterministic-algo + F5-TTS-base; AI is the voice cloner, not the dial driver. The Well-Tuned badge applies to the published v8 LoRA regardless of which mode the user picks at the UI.
- **Workflows:** Mondegreen recipe synthesis [`w1azyqd0f`](#) (3 probes, all surfaced supporting evidence; workflow synthesis itself was NOT independently audited for cited-source faithfulness ‚Äî that's why this entry hedges aggressively on the secondary citations). Earlier citation audit [`wtq6yi1zc`](#) (which refuted 6/10 of v8's original cited sources) is the precedent for that hedging.

## 2026-06-11 ‚Äî Phoneme substitution kernel: PanPhon Boltzmann √ó dreamy_weight (replaces bigram-conditional draw)

- **Choice:** Replace the bigram-conditional class-masked draw in `scripts/corrupt_phonemes.py:corrupt()` with a Boltzmann distribution over the 39 ARPAbet phonemes:
  ```
  q(y | x, level) ‚àù exp( -D[x,y] / T(level) ) * bias_weight(y)
  T(level) = 0.5 * exp(2.5 * p_level)   # T(0)=0.50, T(2)=1.75, T(4)=6.09
  ```
  `D` is the 39√ó39 PanPhon feature-edit-distance matrix, computed at LM-build time and stored in `data/phoneme_lm.npz`. `bias_weight` is the per-phoneme multiplier from the active preset (`dreamy` by default). At lv3-4 we also apply CV cluster simplification (collapse CC onset runs).
- **Rejected:** (a) Keep the bigram-conditional draw ‚Äî produces phonetically arbitrary substitutions; v7 ear-test confirmed lv4 audio sounded like a different sentence, not a phonetic ghost. (b) Use Hirjee & Brown 2010 mondegreen confusion matrix ‚Äî refuted by workflow `wtrmaydq7` (mondegreens are lexically motivated; would produce misheard English words at lv2, opposite of glossolalia). (c) Miller & Nicely 1955 consonant confusion matrix ‚Äî only 16 consonants, noise-condition-specific, no vowel data.
- **What this gives us vs. v7:** at lv0 the lyric stays nearly identity (T=0.5 puts ~98% weight on self for most phonemes); at lv2 substitutions stay within Hamming distance 2-3 (P‚ÜîB, S‚ÜîSH, F‚ÜîV ‚Äî near-minimal phonetic pairs); at lv4 the distribution opens and bias_weight steers the attractor toward sonorants/voiced fricatives/open back vowels. Local diverse-input test verified syllable count is preserved across cluster-heavy ("Strange tides crash"), vowel-heavy ("Oh how I love"), proper-name ("Eleanor walked through Aberdeen"), and long-form ("The endless rain falls softly...") inputs at all 5 levels.
- **What survives verification, what doesn't:**
  - **PanPhon distance values verified** by direct measurement against the installed library: P/B=1, S/SH=2, P/M=3, P/ZH=7, K/N=8, AA/P=11 ‚Äî all match. Matrix range [0, 48].
  - **F5-TTS character-mode confirmed** by issue #362 (SWivid, owner): "current base models are using characters rather than phonemes" ‚Äî pseudo-ASCII input is in-distribution.
  - **CV preference grounded** in [Link & Tomaschek 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10916350/) (95.7% CV in 7,486 German-L1 glossolalia syllables; top-6 syllables `[na, ra, la, ja, ba, da]`) AND in [Samarin 1973 *Language and Speech* 16:1](https://archive.org/details/tonguesofmenange0000sama/) ("preference for open syllables" as one of four formal features of glossolalia). This justifies CV cluster simplification at lv3-4.
  - **T(level) exponential schedule** is a design choice. No published precedent for this exact formula on TTS phoneme corruption; chosen by feel + smoke-tested on diverse inputs.
  - **`bias_weight` (dreamy preset) values are a design heuristic.** Earlier comments overclaimed Link & Tomaschek 2024 + Crystal 1995 as sources for the multipliers ‚Äî neither paper publishes per-phoneme frequency tables, and Samarin's own onset data shows obstruent dominance (~79%) over sonorants (~19%), directly contradicting any "sonorant-heavy" provenance claim. The multipliers are hand-tuned by the author for a soft/sustained aesthetic. See correction below to the 2026-06-01 entry.
  - **PanPhon paper provenance is honest now:** the library exposes 24 features (3 added post the original COLING 2016 paper which had 21) and uses an external ARPAbet‚ÜíIPA conversion (handled by us). Cited as a library, not as theoretical validation.
  - **Concept Sliders r=4 Œ±=1 1000 iter recipe** (used to inform v6) targets SD-UNet, not DiT ‚Äî informed prior, not validated transfer. v6 empirical refutation (0/9 audible) confirmed the architecture difference matters.
- **Sources (only what was verified against the primary):** PanPhon library (Mortensen et al., COLING 2016, [aclanthology.org/C16-1328](https://aclanthology.org/C16-1328/)) ‚Äî used as software; Link & Tomaschek 2024 ([PMC10916350](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10916350/)) ‚Äî CV figure and top-6 syllables, NOT per-phoneme multipliers; Samarin 1973 *Language and Speech* 16:1 ‚Äî CV preference; F5-TTS issue [#362](https://github.com/SWivid/F5-TTS/issues/362) (SWivid) ‚Äî character input mode. ## 2026-06-11 ‚Äî CORRECTION to 2026-06-01 "Phoneme LM rebias toward dreamy / wordless-vocal palette"

The 2026-06-01 entry implied that the dreamy preset's per-phoneme multipliers (M=1.6, L=1.5, AA=1.8, etc.) were derived from Link & Tomaschek 2024 + Pattison 1968 + Bryant & O'Connell 1971 + Crystal 1995. Citation audit `wtq6yi1zc` 2026-06-11 refuted that:

- Link & Tomaschek 2024 documents syllable *structure* (95.7% CV) and top-6 *syllable types* `[na, ra, la, ja, ba, da]` ‚Äî but does NOT publish per-phoneme frequency tables. The multipliers are not derivable from this source.
- Samarin's onset-consonant frequency data (the paper *is* Samarin 1973, *Language and Speech* 16:1, not 1972 *Tongues of Men and Angels*) shows obstruents ~79% vs sonorants ~19% in onset position ‚Äî *contradicting* the "sonorant-heavy" framing.
- Crystal 1995 "Phonaesthetically Speaking" favors *closed* mid-to-high vowels for aesthetic pleasantness, not open vowels ‚Äî *contradicts* the "open vowel = dreamy" framing.

Honest provenance for the dreamy preset multipliers (and the `sigur-ros` and `fraser` presets):

> Hand-tuned design heuristic. The author chose to boost sonorants and open back vowels and suppress voiceless stops/affricates because that palette sounds soft and sustained to the author's ear. The values are not derived from a published frequency table. Samarin's CV preference grounds the syllable-shape preservation; the per-phoneme weighting beyond CV is aesthetic choice.

This honest provenance now lives in [scripts/build_phoneme_lm.py](scripts/build_phoneme_lm.py) lines 50-70 (the docstring for BIAS_PRESETS).

## 2026-06-01 ‚Äî Phoneme LM rebias toward dreamy / wordless-vocal palette  *(in flight)*
